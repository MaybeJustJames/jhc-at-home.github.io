I applied a statistical inference technique known as "Minimum Message Length" to the problem of aligning protein structures. Alignment is the assignment of amino acid residue-residue correspondencdes. A good alignment is biologically meaningful but this comes down to a trade-off between how closely the structures match and the number of assigned correspondences. The Minimum Message Length technique combines statistical inference and data compression. It provides a rigorous basis for hypothesis testing. If an alignment is a hypothesis on the relationship between 2 structures, I should be able to use it to compress the structural data of the 2 structures into a shorter message than I can with no alignment at all. So the best alignment is the alignment that gives me the shortest (most compressed) message. So I have a big interest in data compression. I also have a big interest in statistical inference.
